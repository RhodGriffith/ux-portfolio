Primary colours

Our primary palette is comprised of red, black and white.

Red is our brand colour and is one of our key distinctive elements, use it sparingly for key call to actions on a page. Large areas of red such as heros and banners should be avoided as red is also used to communicate errors on a page.

Design guidelines

Color is an important asset in design of content, enhancing its aesthetic appeal, usability and accessibility. However, some users have difficulty perceiving color.

Don't use colour alone to convey meaning

Designs must be meaningful without colour. Non-visual and visual users must be taken into account when using color to communicate content and data. Content and data communicated with color must also be communicated through other means to ensure it's accessible to all users.

In this example, splitting follow up actions into two separate lists and communicating their relevence would make the content more meaningful and accessible to users.

Use colour sparingly

Overuse of colour can create a cognitive overload and detracts from the strength of the benefits of color. Use colour intelligently to draw emphasis to elements and to reinforce hierarchy.

Accessibility

Colour contrast

Colour contrast has a huge impact on accessibility. WCAG 2.0 level AA requires a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text (18px bold or larger). WCAG 2.1 requires a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 for graphics and user interface components (such as form input borders).
