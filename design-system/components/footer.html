Design guidelines

Anatomy of a footer

  1. Copyright information

    Required copyright information

  2. Links

    Use the footer to provide links to content which you may not want to include in the primary navigation of a product site. These could include links to required legal information or customer support pages on the LexisNexis Risk website.

  3. RELX branding

    It's a requirement to include RELX branding in the footer on all public facing pages. The logo should provide a link the the main RELX website which opens in a new window.

Accessibility

Page regions

Similar to the page header, all products will have have a region at the bottom of every page that contains site-wide information, such as copyright information, privacy statements, legal information, or customer support links. HTML5 provides the <footer> element, which can be used to define such a region.

Footer navigation

All navigation elements should have a <nav> tag. If you have to use a more generic element such as a <div> , add role="navigation" to every navbar to explicitly identify it as a landmark region for users of assistive technologies.

Menus should be labeled according to their individual function. Use class="visuallyhidden", aria-label="", or aria-labelledby="" to easily add context to the different menus on your site.
