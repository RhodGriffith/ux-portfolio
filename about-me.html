About me

Hi, I’m Rhodri and I’ve worked in the design industry since graduating from the University of South Wales in 2001. With an agency background in graphic design, experience working as a digital designer within government and a recent work history as a user experience designer in the private sector, I’m highly skilled in every aspect of the design process.

My core skills revolve around user centred design – creative problem-solving with a scientific and evidence based approach to design. I’m a firm believer in designing products and services that are centred around user needs. Clean, functional design, paying attention to the finest detail.

Career history

Senior UX Designer – Lexis Nexis Risk Solutions

November 2019 to present day

I lead the UX function at Lexis Nexis working across multiple web based products and mobile applications. I work with teams who build digital identity, tracing, risk and compliance products – sold primarily in the financial services, insurance and pensions sectors.

Being the first UX hire within the company, I plan and conduct research activities across our product portfolio, design prototypes to test with users as well as shaping the products from a visual design, usability and accessibility perspective.

UX Designer – Motokiki

March 2019 to September 2019

I joining Motokiki at an early stage of the start up venture – a comparison site for tyres providing consumers with an impartial data across a wide range of retailers. My main responsibilities were centred around optimising the user journey to maximise conversion on the ecommerce website. I analysed data collected through analytics and research tools – primarily Hot Jar and Google Analytics – to provide insight into the behaviour of users. This insight was subsequently used to iterate user flows, improve usability and prototype new features. I also planned A/B experiments and championed UX best and accessibility standards within the company.

Senior Interaction Designer – Office for National Statistics

October 2017 to March 2019

I worked within an agile, multi-disciplined team to deliver highly user focussed and successful services such as the ONS Business Register and 2021 Census. I was responsible for translating findings from user research into design concepts, interactive prototypes and worked with front end developers to implement consistently.

Digital Design Lead – Office for National Statistics

July 2015 to October 2017

I was responsible for coordinating the production of design and data visualisation work across a range of digital media. I managed a team of six designers to deliver a range of products to connect ONS data with its users in innovative, creative and effective ways. My design work was regularly used by the wider media (Guardian, BBC) in their story-telling and data journalism activities reaching millions of users.

Freelance Designer

November 2010 to September 2017

In 2010, I setup my own freelance business whilst working full time at ONS. This enabled me to progress as a designer, learn new skills and pursuing my passion for the web and developing brands. I formed a close partnership with a marketing agency in Cowbridge called Jamjar. I worked on a number of high profile public and private sector projects for organisations including Sport Wales, Wales Millennium Centre, NHS, Merthyr Council, Techniquest, Cardiff University, Sony and Ffos Las Racecourse.

Graphic and Web Designer – Freshwater UK

August 2001 to December 2007

Freshwater, a marketing and design agency was my first design job out of university. At the time, it was a dream job, providing great opportunity to work for a number of high profile brands such as HSBC and Morphy Rochards.
